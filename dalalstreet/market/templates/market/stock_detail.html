📚 Understanding the fundamentals
{% if stock.pe_ratio %}
P/E Ratio — {{ stock.pe_ratio }}
+
What is P/E ratio? It compares the stock price to the company's earnings per share. A P/E of {{ stock.pe_ratio }} means investors are paying ₹{{ stock.pe_ratio }} for every ₹1 of profit the company makes. {% if stock.pe_ratio > 40 %}

⚠️ High P/E alert: A P/E above 40 suggests the market expects very strong future growth. If that growth doesn't happen, the stock could fall significantly. {% elif stock.pe_ratio < 15 %}

✅ Low P/E: This could mean the stock is undervalued — or the market expects slower growth. Always research why before buying. {% else %}

✅ Moderate P/E: This is within a reasonable range for most sectors.{% endif %}
{% endif %} {% if stock.eps %}
EPS — ₹{{ stock.eps }}
+
What is EPS? Earnings Per Share — the profit the company made divided by total shares. {{ stock.company_name }} earned ₹{{ stock.eps }} per share. Higher EPS generally means a more profitable company. Consistent EPS growth over years is a sign of a healthy business.
{% endif %} {% if stock.roe %}
ROE — {{ stock.roe }}%
+
What is ROE? Return on Equity — how efficiently the company uses shareholders' money to generate profit. An ROE of {{ stock.roe }}% means for every ₹100 of shareholder money, the company generates ₹{{ stock.roe }} in profit. {% if stock.roe > 20 %}

✅ Excellent ROE: Above 20% is considered strong. This company is using capital very efficiently. {% elif stock.roe > 12 %}

✅ Good ROE: Between 12-20% is healthy for most industries. {% else %}

⚠️ Low ROE: Below 12% may indicate the company is not generating strong returns on investment.{% endif %}
{% endif %} {% if stock.pb_ratio %}
P/B Ratio — {{ stock.pb_ratio }}
+
What is P/B ratio? Price to Book ratio compares the stock price to the company's book value (assets minus liabilities). A P/B of {{ stock.pb_ratio }} means you are paying ₹{{ stock.pb_ratio }} for every ₹1 of the company's net assets. {% if stock.pb_ratio < 1 %}

✅ Below book value: The stock trades below its asset value — could be undervalued or the market has concerns. {% elif stock.pb_ratio > 5 %}

⚠️ High P/B: The market values this company much higher than its assets — usually justified by strong brand, earnings or growth expectations.{% endif %}
{% endif %} {% if stock.debt_to_equity %}
Debt to Equity — {{ stock.debt_to_equity }}
+
What is Debt to Equity? It shows how much debt the company has compared to shareholder equity. A ratio of {{ stock.debt_to_equity }} means for every ₹1 of equity, the company has ₹{{ stock.debt_to_equity }} of debt. {% if stock.debt_to_equity < 0.5 %}

✅ Low debt: This company has very little debt — financially stable and lower risk. {% elif stock.debt_to_equity > 2 %}

⚠️ High debt: High debt increases financial risk, especially during economic downturns. Check if the company generates enough cash to service its debt.{% endif %}
{% endif %}

🔴 Small cap (below ₹5,000 Cr): Early stage or niche companies. High risk, high reward potential.
